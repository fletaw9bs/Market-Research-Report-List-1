<p><ul><li>Physical Method</li><li>Chemical Method</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Activated carbon powders are produced primarily through two methods: physical and chemical. The physical method involves carbonization, where raw materials like wood or coal are heated in an inert atmosphere, followed by activation with steam or CO2, enhancing porosity. The chemical method, on the other hand, involves treating precursors with chemical agents like phosphoric acid or potassium hydroxide, which increases surface area and pore structure. Each method influences the properties and applications of activated carbon powders, catering to diverse market needs.</p></p>
